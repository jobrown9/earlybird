Our top 10 recommended places to visit in Provence

 

 
 

 

 

 

 

Salernes en Provence

The village of Salernes is renowned for its Sunday market and picturesque appeal. It also offers a great number of activities. You can visit its ruined feudal castle overlooking the village or go down to the roman bath. A section of the river that has been landscaped into a local swimming and sunbathing spot. On the outskirts of the village you can find the Saint Barthlemy valley, which offers shaded peaceful walks, a number of caves and an ancient Templar chapel.

 

 

 

 

Sillans la cascade

This beautiful Provençale village has a breath-taking waterfall and an impressive fortified chateau surrounding part of the village that has retained all of its local charm. It also has an extremely good restaurant, excellent walking and cycling routes along the old railway that runs parallel to the river.

 

 

 

 

 

Villecroze

Another typical Provençale village that becomes very active in summer, on some weekends it offering outdoor plays, a number of bars and restaurants, and beautiful gardens that go with a fortified castle set into the side of a cliff that is truly impressive.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Aups

This is a bustling small town with excellent Wednesday and Saturday Provencale markets, it also has a range of shops, bars, restaurants, and an incredibly good Boulangerie that is known for miles around. There are also a number of summer festivals and fairs taking place here during the summer.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Tourtour

Known as the village in the sky, is another typical village with breath-taking views of the valleys below, it offers very pleasant walks and a number of bars and restaurants.

 

 

 

 

 

 

The lake Saint Croix

This huge lake that feels more like a small ocean offers countless activities. It's possible to rent windsurfing boards, quatamarans (without any prior experience necesary), canoes, and kayaks. The lake also has the 2nd largest gorge in the world (Known as Europe’s grand canyon - and locally as 'Gorge Du Verdon') can be found at one end of the lake. Here it is possible to rent canoes, pedaloes and kayaks to go up the gorge.

It is possible to travel upstream for more than 40 minutes surrounded by really impressive sheer walls on each side. You can also go bungee jumping off one the bridges above the lake, follow the countless bike trails that go all the way around the lake or simply go for a swim and do a bit of sunbathing.

 

 

St Tropez

Originally a picturesque fishing village, now home to very large motor yachts, a few very good restaurants, outdoor cafes, a range of shops and a large display of work by local artists in the harbour.

Note that getting to St Tropez can always be tricky, and that the easiest way to visit the town is to catch a 'bateau verts' ferry in Sainte Maxime. This takes you to Saint Tropez harbour. (Adult tickets are around 6€). In the middle of the summer the roads leading up to the town get completely jammers and it would typically take hours to cover the short distance along the coast.

 

 

Cannes
This is a bustling town ideal for shopping, lying around on beaches, going for adventurous walks around the windy streets or enjoying a spot of theatre. You can also walk up the red carpet of the Cannes Festival. The easiest way to get into Cannes is by train, which takes out the hassle of trying to find parking around the town.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Aix-en-Provence

This famous old roman town is full of history and beautiful old monuments, known by the French as 'Le Petit Paris', it offers a very relaxed atmosphere, a large number of pedestrian shopping streets, very good restaurants, theatres, museums, etc.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Monte Carlo/Monaco

If you are on the lookout for large yachts and fast cars this is the place for you. It also offers a superb oceanographic museum and a large casino.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Porquerolle Island

If you have the time this place should not be missed, it offers mind blowing scenery and incredible beaches. Its the idea spot for a day of walking or cycling around this natural reserve that is packed full of history. There are over 54Km of walking and cycling trails. If you are then looking to relax the only village on the island offers a number of quality bars and restaurants. The ferry leaves from Hyeres and takes about 20 minutes (price is around 18 Euros for a return)
