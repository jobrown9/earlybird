The village - Salernes en Provence

 

 

The village of Salernes, situated in the heart of Provence, is located in the sheltered valley of the Bresque river, surrounded by pine and oak forests, olive groves and vineyards. During the middle ages it was a thriving agricultural community, producing wine, olive oil, wheat and fruit.


Then, at the beginning of the 19th century it was discovered to have an exceptional iron-rich clay soil, leading to the production of the well-known Provençal  'tomette', a red hexagonal floor tile. To this day the village continues to produce 'tomettes' and more recently, ceramic tiles and a wider selection of terracotta floor tiles.


It is now a bustling community with lively markets twice a week - Salernes' Sunday market is one of the most popular in the region - together with excellent shopping facilities, bakeries, 2 chemists and several supermarkets.
During the warm summer months regular musical evenings in the village square are very popular, creating a typical Mediterranean atmosphere, with bars and restaurants jostling for space under the plane trees.  ‘Flea markets’ are now a regular event and a good selection of sports and activities are on offer in and around the village, including tennis, horse riding, swimming, fishing and golf. Ski resorts of the lower Alps are within a two-hour drive. There is a decent modern cinema showing the latest films.


The towns of Aix-en-Provence, Cannes, Nice, St Tropez and Monte Carlo are all easy day visits.
Nice and Marseille airports are 1¼ hrs and 1½ hrs respectively.

Local activities

Cycling and walking are both popular outdoor activities in the area with plenty of scope along kilometres of scenic routes away from the main roads. Tennis and horse riding can be found in and around the village and golf 25 minutes by car.

 

 

The Lac St Croix is a refreshing change to the busy coast in summer, where one can picnic, swim, windsurf, sail or canoe in impressive and peaceful surroundings.

 

For the more adventurous, canyoning, white water rafting, kayaking, bungee jumping and rock climbing in the spectacular Gorges du Verdon, Europe's deepest gorge, which runs into the Lac St Croix.

 

Or drive up through the lavender fields and practice some hang gliding in the stunning location of St-Andre-Les Alpes, a world class flying site.

Surrounding villages /About the area

It is surrounded by other smaller villages such as Tourtour (15mns), known as the village in the sky because it has commanding views over a large area, Cotignac which is known for its scenic setting and markets, Lorgues which is known for its vineyards, Sillans la Cascade (10mns) with is known for its fortifications and impressive waterfall, and Entrecasteaux which is known for its castle set in a cliff and Aups which is known for its summer festivals, its excellent Provencale Saturday market and shopping.
