What's on in Provence

 

April

Bull fighting festival in Arles (18th April)
This marks the beginning of the bull-fighting season.

 

May

Roman games in Nimes (17th & 18th May)
This includes performances, re-enactments and entertainments in the ancient roman Nimes Arena.

Luberon Jazz festival (27th & 28th May)
Noted for its new generation of French jazz players.

 

June

Fete de la Bravade in Saint Tropez (First week of June - and end of may)
This is a religious festival with music and dancing.

Summer solstice
Most villages have traditional dancing going on - each village will have something to offer, but the general idea is that the locals dress up in traditional costume and gather in the main square for music and dancing in the evening. Bonfires are then lit and the party lasts well into the night.

 

July

Festival D'Aix (Takes place during whole month of July)
Is a huge arts festival with opera, concerts and orchestras.

Festival D'Avignon (Whole month of July)
Another very large arts festival held during the whole of July.

Tour de France (5th to 27th July)
The whole region is gripped by this race as it shoots through villages. A common route for the tour is through the village of Vilecroze which is a 10 minute drive from Salernes. This year (2014) is the 100th anniversary of the Tour so it will bigger than ever. Check here for routes & dates.

Bastille Day/French National Day (14th July)
This is celebrated throughout every village with a bang. Quality fireworks are on show in Aix-en-Provence, Nice and Cannes. Salernes also offers one of the largest fireworks displays in the region.

Visit the lavender fields (whole of July)
July tend to be the optimal flowering period for Lavender

 

August

The agricultural and artisanale fair in Aups (August)
This involves groups of locals dressing up in traditional dress.

Summer fair and fun fair in Aups (15th to 18th August)
This fair has a whole range of rides and concerts on offer.

The regional Boule competition in Aups (21st to 22nd August)
Known as the Grand Prix de la ville d'Aups au jeu provencal. This is a fun way to get involved with the locals and experience Provence first hand.

 

September

Fete de la Vendange (Grape picking) takes place in Saint Tropez - which is followed straight after by the St Tropez sailing Regatta at the end of the month. (stats 29th of September) beginning of October.

Grape picking starts during the month of October and there is no better way to experience the place than by getting your hands dirty. Its incredibly easy to go and do a days worth of picking. Just drive up to one of the local chateau's and they'll take you on for the day.

 

October

Sailing Regatta in Saint Tropez (29th October to 4th October)
Very large competition with a whole range of classic sailing boats. (known as the 'Les Voiles de St Tropez').
